## Course Overview

This course introduces students to the rapidly evolving field of **Agentic AI** — artificial intelligence systems that can perceive their environment, make decisions, take actions, and learn from outcomes with minimal human intervention. Students will explore the foundational concepts behind autonomous agents, understand modern architectures like ReAct, tool-use, and multi-agent systems, and build working AI agents using current frameworks and tools.

By the end of this course, students will be able to design, build, and evaluate AI agents that can reason, plan, use tools, and collaborate with other agents to accomplish complex tasks.

---

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. Define what Agentic AI is and distinguish it from traditional AI approaches.
2. Explain core agent architectures including ReAct, Plan-and-Execute, and Reflexion.
3. Understand how Large Language Models (LLMs) serve as the reasoning engine for modern AI agents.
4. Implement tool-use patterns that allow agents to interact with external systems.
5. Design and build single-agent systems that can reason, plan, and act autonomously.
6. Architect multi-agent systems where specialized agents collaborate on complex tasks.
7. Apply memory and retrieval mechanisms (short-term, long-term, RAG) to agent systems.
8. Evaluate agent performance, handle failure modes, and implement safety guardrails.
9. Identify real-world applications and ethical considerations of deploying agentic systems.
